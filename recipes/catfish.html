Southern Fried Catfish

Description

This Southern Fried Catfish is golden brown on the outside and flaky and delicious on the inside. Seasoned through and through, it's the perfect recipe for when you want a good piece of fried fish. Fried fish is a staple among the Black community in the United States and it's a vital part of African-American culinary traditions. Many celebrations call for a fish fry where pieces of fish are seasoned and deep-fried until golden brown.

Ingredients

  • Catfish filets
  • Cornmeal
  • Flour
  • Cajun seasoning
  • Seasoned salt
  • Garlic powder
  • Onion powder
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Add about 4-5 inches of oil to a heavy-bottomed pot and heat over medium-high heat until the temperature of the oil reaches 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In one shallow bowl, add cornmeal, flour, seasonings, and whisk to combine.
  3. Dip the fillets into the seasoned cornmeal mixture, shaking off any excess cornmeal.
  4. Gently drop the catfish fillets into the hot oil. Fry each filet for about 3-4 minutes until the fillets are golden brown.
  5. Remove the filets with a slotted spoon and lay them on a wire rack or paper towels.
  6. Serve the filets as is or season with additional salt and pepper.
